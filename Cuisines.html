Indian Cuisines

The traditional food of India has been widely appreciated for its fabulous use of herbs and spices. Indian cuisine is known for its large assortment of dishes. The cooking style varies from region to region. Indian cuisine is one of the most diverse in the world, characterized by its sophisticated and subtle use of spices, grains, vegetables, and fruits.

Bengali Food

Bengali cuisine is appreciated for its fabulous use of panchphoron, a term used to refer to the five essential spices: mustard, fenugreek seed, cumin seed, aniseed, and black cumin seed. The simplicity of Bengali food lies in the perfect blend of sweet and spicy flavors.

Gujarati Food

The traditional Gujarati food is mostly vegetarian and has a high nutritional value. The typical Gujarati thali consists of roti, dal or kadhi, rice, and sabzi or shaak. Gujarati cuisine is one that is known for its distinct taste with its use of sweet and salty flavors.

Mughlai Food

Mughlai cuisine is one of the most popular cuisines, whose origin can be traced back to the time of Mughal Empire. Mughlai dishes are rich and spicy and are cooked with a lot of cream and spices. Mughal Emperors introduced elaborate cooking styles that involve slow cooking and rich gravies like Mughlai kofta, biryanis, and chicken tikka.

Punjabi Food

The cuisine of Punjab has an enormous variety of mouth-watering vegetarian as well as non-vegetarian dishes. The spice content ranges from minimal to pleasant to high. Home cooking differs from restaurant-style cooking significantly. In Punjab, home cooking starts from the freshest ingredients and traditional methods.

South Indian Cuisine

The cuisine of South India is known for its light, low-calorie appetizing dishes. The traditional food of South India is mainly rice-based. The food is a perfect blend of flavors and colors prepared using tamarind, coconut, mustard seeds, curry leaves, and red chilies.
